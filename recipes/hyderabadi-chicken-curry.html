Chicken Biryani

Description>

Chicken biryani is a classic South Asian one-pot dish featuring marinated chicken layered with fragrant basmati rice, slow-cooked together (often using the dum method) with spices, herbs, yogurt, and saffron. It is known for its aromatic, flavorful, and tender texture, commonly garnished with crispy fried onions, mint, and cilantro.

Ingredients

  • Chicken & Marinade: 1-1.5 lb bone-in chicken thighs/drumsticks, yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric, garam masala, and lemon juice.
  • Rice: Long-grain Basmati rice.
  • Whole Spices: Bay leaves, green/black cardamom, cloves, cinnamon stick, star anise, cumin seeds.
  • Aromatics: Red or yellow onions (thinly sliced and fried until crispy), garlic, ginger, green chilies.
  • Herbs & Liquids: Fresh mint leaves, cilantro (coriander), ghee or oil, saffron threads soaked in warm milk.
  • Fried Onions (Barista): Critical for flavor and texture.

Steps

1. Marinate the Chicken

  1. Prepare Marinade: In a large bowl, mix yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric, red chilli powder, biryani masala (or garam masala), lemon juice, and salt.
  2. Coat Chicken: Add chicken pieces (preferably bone-in for better flavor) and coat them thoroughly with the marinade.
  3. Rest: Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes, though 2 hours to overnight is ideal for the most tender results.

2. Prepare the Aromatics & Chicken Curry

  1. Fry Onions: Heat ghee or oil in a heavy-bottomed pot. Fry thinly sliced onions until they are a deep golden brown (this is called birista). Remove half of the onions and set aside for layering.
  2. Sauté Spices: In the same oil, add whole spices (bay leaf, cinnamon, cardamom, cloves, and star anise) until they sizzle and become fragrant.
  3. Cook Chicken: Add the marinated chicken to the pot. Sauté for 5–8 minutes on medium-high heat until the chicken changes color.
  4. Simmer: Add a splash of water, cover, and cook on low heat until the chicken is roughly 80-90% cooked and a thick gravy remains.

3. Par-boil the Basmati Rice

  1. Wash & Soak: Rinse aged basmati rice several times until the water runs clear, then soak it in fresh water for 30 minutes.
  2. Boil Water: Bring a large pot of water to a rolling boil with salt and whole spices (shahi jeera, cardamom, cloves).
  3. Cook Rice: Add the soaked rice and boil for about 5 minutes. The rice should be 70-80% cooked—it should be firm with a slight "bite" in the middle and not mushy.
  4. Drain: Immediately drain the rice in a colander and set aside.

4. Layering and Dum Cooking

  1. Layer 1 (Bottom): Keep the cooked chicken and its thick gravy at the bottom of the heavy pot.
  2. Layer 2 (Rice): Spread the par-boiled rice evenly over the chicken.
  3. Add Toppings: Sprinkle the reserved fried onions, fresh mint, and coriander leaves. Drizzle saffron-infused milk and a tablespoon of ghee over the top for aroma and color.
  4. Seal & Steam (Dum): Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid or seal it with aluminum foil/dough to trap the steam.
  5. Final Cook: Cook on very low heat for 20–25 minutes. For even heat distribution, you can place the pot on a flat griddle (tawa).
  6. Rest: Turn off the heat and let the biryani rest for 10 minutes before opening and fluffing the rice with a fork.
